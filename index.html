About

I'm a Research Scientist with a background in both Molecular Biology and Bioinformatics. I obtained my PhD degree and postdoctoral training in Dr. Craig Kaplan's group at University of Pittsburgh (Pitt). During my graduate school study, I dissected Ssl2/TFIIH protein's function in RNA Polymerase II (Pol II) transcription start site (TSS) selection using yeast genetics and NGS methods. After Pitt, I joined Brigham and Women's Hospital as a Research Scientist in Bioinformatics and worked on next-generation sequencing (NGS) data analysis, including single-cell RNA-sequencing data analysis. My experience involves both dry lab and wet lab skills (moist). Currently, I'm highly interested in using various technologies to solve biological questions that people are most caring about.

Skills

The highlights of my skills are in molecular biology and NGS data analysis. I've also trained by the best Geneticist, my PhD advisor Dr. Craig Kaplan, to learn the power and beauty of the Genetics in solving biological questions. In addition, I love teaching and enjoy the process of interpreting and sharing research with others.
